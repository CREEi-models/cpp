    \section{Calling CPP Fortran Module from
Python}\label{calling-cpp-fortran-module-from-python}

    We use f2py to pre-compile our fortran cpp module (available at
https://github.com/CEDIA-models/cpp). We can then use the module (all
its functions and global variables) interactively from Python. Here is
an example.

    \begin{Verbatim}[commandchars=\\\{\}]
{\color{incolor}In [{\color{incolor}35}]:} \PY{k+kn}{from} \PY{n+nn}{cpp} \PY{k}{import} \PY{n}{cpp}
\end{Verbatim}


    Now load the parameters from cpp

    \begin{Verbatim}[commandchars=\\\{\}]
{\color{incolor}In [{\color{incolor}36}]:} \PY{n}{cpp}\PY{o}{.}\PY{n}{loadcpp}\PY{p}{(}\PY{p}{)}\PY{p}{;}
\end{Verbatim}


    Now let's create an individual age 60 who is considering retiring.

    \begin{Verbatim}[commandchars=\\\{\}]
{\color{incolor}In [{\color{incolor}37}]:} \PY{k+kn}{import} \PY{n+nn}{numpy} \PY{k}{as} \PY{n+nn}{np}

         \PY{n}{age} \PY{o}{=} \PY{l+m+mi}{60}\PY{p}{;}
\end{Verbatim}


    Now let's setup is earnings history. The regime is in place since 1966.
Let's assume this retiree has contributed the maximum in each year
between 1968 and 2010 (1968 is the year he turned 18).

    \begin{Verbatim}[commandchars=\\\{\}]

he was to claim at a different age, assuming he does not work and
